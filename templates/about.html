About Heart Health Check

Understanding cardiovascular health and our AI-powered assessment system

What is Cardiovascular Disease?

Cardiovascular disease refers to a range of conditions that affect the heart and blood vessels. It remains the leading cause of death globally, often developing silently over many years. Common types include coronary artery disease, heart failure, arrhythmias, and hypertension-related complications.

Major Risk Factors

🩺 High Blood Pressure
🧈 High Cholesterol
🍬 Diabetes
🚬 Smoking & Alcohol
⚖️ Obesity
🏃‍♂️ Lack of Physical Activity
👴 Age
🧬 Family History

Why Early Detection Matters

Early identification of cardiovascular risk factors allows for timely preventive interventions and lifestyle modifications. Machine learning algorithms can detect subtle patterns in health data that might be missed by traditional assessment methods, enabling proactive healthcare management.

About This AI Project

Heart Health Check is a machine learning-based system that analyzes cardiovascular risk using comprehensive medical and lifestyle data. The system employs advanced algorithms to provide personalized risk assessments and health insights.

Dataset: Cardiovascular disease prediction dataset with 70,000+ patient records from medical examinations.

Features: Age, gender, height, weight, blood pressure (systolic/diastolic), cholesterol levels, glucose levels, smoking status, alcohol consumption, and physical activity.

Preprocessing: Age conversion from days to years, BMI calculation, feature scaling using StandardScaler.

Model Training: Multiple algorithms compared including Logistic Regression, KNN, Decision Tree, Random Forest, Gradient Boosting, and Naive Bayes variants.

Why Gradient Boosting?

Gradient Boosting achieved the highest accuracy (73.8%) in our evaluation due to its ensemble learning approach that combines multiple weak learners sequentially. Each new model corrects the errors of the previous ones, leading to a strong predictive model that effectively handles complex feature interactions and provides robust performance on medical prediction tasks.
